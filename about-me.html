Who am I?

Having started my academic life as an engineer, I have desired to become an engineer who works with assembling new materials and uses engineering skills to become a so-called ‘engineer’. Nevertheless, life did not allow me to persist with my passion, and due to an accident, I lost 50% of my dominant hand power.
Due to medical circumstances, doctors have asked me to change my mind and ask if I want to be more effective, so I can choose another field of study and continue my academic adventure. Although it was a challenging determination, I continued my pilgrimage through medical imaging studies because of my background in the medical field and my short interest in medical imaging, including CT scans and MRIs. Regardless, this was different from the point at which I felt satisfied, so I determined to do much research, explore the basics, share knowledge on the Internet, and choose a field I liked. After many searches and spending days and days of my life, I finally decided to choose ‘cognitive science’ as my major.

The brain, and particularly the human brain, is the most complex and still not well-known structure in our world. How much do we know about neurons, cells, connections, and brain function during different activities? How does our brain determine whether one thing is beautiful, a stressful situation, or we are in love with someone?


Although most of the above questions are not my research field, these and related questions motivated me to choose cognitive neuroscientists and psychophysics researchers. Although clinical psychologists are super lovely people, sitting and talking to patients was not my passion, I wanted to use my previous knowledge, such as math, brain imaging, and programming, in a more active way rather than just diagnosis based on therapy sessions, so I chose to become a more clinical researcher.
In 2019 after many years of preparation, I started my professional “Cognitive neuroscience” studies at UFU, and I am on my way to becoming a researcher. Since 2018 professionally, I have devoted my life to learning, analyzing, and having well-structured critical thinking in neuroscience. I continued my academic career as a researcher at the Neurotechnology Laboratory at UFU and have done my project under the title of
“attention and memory after ischemic stroke in children under 18 years of age: a cognitive neuroimaging approach led by Professor S. Kiselev from UFU.
A year later, in 2021, I joined LingMed lab, which is under the supervision of UMU and UFU. We have collaborated on brain, memory, attention, and emotion regulation using BCIs, including EEG, Eye-tracker, and f/MRI approaches.
At the end of 2022, I joined the University of Padova, Department of General Psychology under a Marie Curie fellowship to carry out a project entitled “Innovative Methods Or Technologies For Assessment, Intervention, Or Enhancement Of Psychological Functions (Cognitive And Emotional)” under the supervision of Prof. Luca Battaglini and incorporate with PercUP lab led by Prof. M. Bertamini and colleagues.
