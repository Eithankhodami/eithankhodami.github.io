Volunteering

Project

Our journey begins with the visual system - the gateway for over 80% of the information we absorb about the world. Like intrepid explorers mapping uncharted territory, we will uncover how the brain makes sense of the constant flood of visual data. How does it transform fragmented light signals into a seamless perception of objects, motion, color and depth? What hidden mechanisms allow us to instantly recognize a familiar face? Our expedition will traverse into the realms of attention, perception, and cognition. Here we will discover the subtle biases that filter our reality, and how deficits in vision can profoundly alter one's experience.

As we venture into the emotional centers of the brain, be prepared for turbulence! This is a frontier filled with mystery and complexity. How does the firing of neurons in the amygdala lead to feelings of joy, sadness or fear? What secrets lie in the neurotransmitters, hormones and neural pathways that regulate our moods? Can we uncover the neural correlates of empathy?

I invite you to join me on this grand quest into the neural cosmos. Who knows what astonishing insights we will uncover into the machinery of the mind? We may just discover how neural activity gives rise to consciousness itself! Our journey promises to be filled with curiosity, awe and delightful surprises. So come, let us explore together! The discoveries we make may just illuminate the human condition and improve lives. Onward, fellow neuro-adventurers!


MSCA Project

We will recruit science along with promising neuroscience expeditions. Together, we will employ cutting-edge techniques like Eye-tracker, EEG, and neuropsychological assessments to map out the complex terrain of the visual system and emotional processing centers. By integrating insights across neuroanatomy, physiology, and psychology, we aim to develop a unified model of visual information flow and identify key neural correlates of emotional states.

This project will facilitate valuable cross-pollination of ideas and skills through secondments, training workshops, and conferences. We intend to provide excellent professional development opportunities for the next generation of neuroscientists while also fostering collaboration across institutes and disciplines.

Ultimately, our groundbreaking discoveries will be shared with the scientific community through high-impact publications and presentations at major conferences. We hope our results will inform therapeutic approaches to treat emotional and neurological disorders. This project promises to significantly advance the field of cognitive neuroscience while training accomplished researchers who will become future leaders in this exciting domain. Let the neuroscience odyssey begin!
