About Me

My academic journey began in engineering, where I developed a passion for materials science and the art of construction. However, life took an unexpected turn after an accident left me with reduced functionality in my dominant hand. Though disappointed, I resolved to redirect my talents, embarking on a new path in medical imaging.

With experience in CT and MRI technology, I was drawn to the complexities of the human brain. The mind has always fascinated me – how neurons interconnect, and how cognition and emotion arise. My curiosity motivated a career pivot to cognitive neuroscience and psychophysics research.

Though the shift meant abandoning hands-on engineering, I found stimulation in applying analytical skills like math and programming to clinical problems. While rewarding, diagnosing patients in therapy proved less fulfilling than actively investigating the brain’s mysteries.

Thus in 2019, after much personal reflection, I commenced studies in cognitive neuroscience at the Ural Federal University (UFU), determined to become a research scientist. Under the mentorship of Professor S. Kiselev, I began contributing to the Neurotechnology Lab, exploring attention and memory deficits in pediatric stroke victims through cognitive neuroimaging.

In 2021, I joined the LinguaMed Lab, a UFU and  Russian Academy of Science collaboration investigating brain-computer interfaces using EEG, eye tracking, and fMRI. Through 2022, I worked with the lab to study relationships between memory, attention, and emotion regulation.

Most recently, I was awarded a Marie Curie Fellowship to conduct research at the University of Padova’s Department of General Psychology. My project focuses on innovative assessment and intervention methods for cognitive and emotional functions. I collaborating with Professor Luca Battaglini and the PercUP Lab under Professor M. Bertamini.

My goal remains to unravel the brain’s mysteries through rigorous empirical study. Each experience along my academic journey has equipped me to contribute meaningful insights as a cognitive neuroscience researcher. Though much is still unknown about the mind and mental processes, I am committed to steady scientific progress that benefits societies worldwide.
